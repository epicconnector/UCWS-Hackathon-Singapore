# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The UCWS Hackathon Singapore community is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all participants, regardless of:

- Age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, national origin, race, religion, sexual identity or orientation
- Years of experience, education level, or socioeconomic status
- Background, expertise, or role

We are dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone.

---

## Our Values

We believe in:
- **Innovation** – Push creative boundaries and think differently
- **Collaboration** – Support fellow builders and share knowledge
- **Integrity** – Honest work and fair competition
- **Diversity** – Welcome and celebrate different perspectives
- **Respect** – Treat all community members with dignity

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants and community members are expected to:

- **Be respectful** in communication and interactions with others
- **Be inclusive** and welcoming to people of all backgrounds
- **Listen actively** and value different viewpoints
- **Give credit** where it's due and acknowledge contributions
- **Provide constructive feedback** when helping others
- **Report concerns** if you witness or experience misconduct
- **Follow the rules** of the hackathon and community guidelines
- **Respect intellectual property** and give proper attribution

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- **Harassment:** Any form of unwelcome conduct, comments, or advances based on protected characteristics
- **Discrimination:** Treating someone unfairly based on their identity or background
- **Intimidation:** Threatening, bullying, or aggressive behavior
- **Abuse:** Verbal, physical, or emotional abuse of any kind
- **Doxing:** Sharing personal information without consent
- **Plagiarism:** Submitting others' work as your own without attribution
- **Cheating:** Violating hackathon rules or using unfair means to gain advantage
- **Disruptive behavior:** Deliberate disruption of events or community interactions
- **Hate speech:** Any content promoting violence or discrimination
- **Sexual harassment:** Unwelcome sexual advances, requests, or other forms of sexual conduct

---

## Reporting & Resolution

### If You Witness or Experience Misconduct

1. **Tell the person** (if safe to do so) that their behavior is unacceptable
2. **Report to organizers** by emailing [lyssssss613@gmail.com](mailto:lyssssss613@gmail.com)
3. **Provide details:**
   - What happened
   - Who was involved
   - When and where it occurred
   - Any witnesses
   - How it affected you

All reports will be taken seriously and handled confidentially.

### Our Response

EPICConnector will:
- Investigate all reports promptly and fairly
- Take appropriate action based on findings
- Support affected individuals
- Maintain confidentiality where possible
- Provide updates on resolution (when appropriate)

**Consequences for violations may include:**
- Warning or reminder of community standards
- Temporary or permanent removal from the event
- Ban from future EPICConnector events
- Legal action in cases of serious misconduct

## Creating an Inclusive Environment

To help make the hackathon a welcoming space for everyone:

- **Use inclusive language** – Avoid gendered terms when not necessary
- **Respect pronouns** – Ask and use people's correct pronouns
- **Be mindful of accessibility** – Ensure resources are accessible to people with disabilities
- **Challenge bias** – Speak up when you notice discriminatory behavior (kindly, when possible)
- **Amplify underrepresented voices** – Create space for diverse perspectives
- **Educate yourself** – Learn about different cultures, experiences, and perspectives

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All hackathon participants and attendees
- Team members and organizers
- Online and in-person interactions
- All official hackathon spaces (forums, social media, Slack, etc.)
- Interactions related to the hackathon or EPICConnector community
